# OPERATING AGREEMENT

This Operating Agreement ("Agreement") of **ELEVATE EDSTRATEGY GROUP, LLC**, a Texas Limited Liability Company (the "Company"), is entered into and effective as of the _____ day of January, 2026, by and among the undersigned Members.

---

# RECITALS

**WHEREAS**, the Members desire to form a Limited Liability Company under the laws of the State of Texas for the purposes set forth herein;

**WHEREAS**, the Members desire to establish their respective rights, duties, and obligations with respect to the Company;

**WHEREAS**, the Members intend for this Agreement to govern the internal affairs of the Company and the relationships among the Members;

**NOW, THEREFORE**, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements contained herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Members agree as follows:

## Section 6.2 Capital Accounts
A separate Capital Account shall be maintained for each Member. Each Member's Capital Account shall be:

(a) **Increased** by:
   - The amount of cash contributed by such Member to the Company
   - The fair market value of property contributed by such Member to the Company
   - The amount of the Company's income and gain allocated to such Member

(b) **Decreased** by:
   - The amount of cash distributed to such Member
   - The fair market value of property distributed to such Member
   - The amount of the Company's losses and deductions allocated to such Member

## Section 6.3 Additional Capital Contributions
(a) No Member shall be required to make any additional Capital Contributions to the Company beyond the initial Capital Contribution set forth in Section 6.1, except as unanimously agreed by all Members.

(b) If additional capital is required for the Company's operations, the Members may unanimously agree to:
   - Make additional pro-rata Capital Contributions
   - Obtain financing from third parties
   - Obtain loans from one or more Members

(a) Any Member may, with the approval of a majority of the other Members, make loans to the Company at such interest rates and on such terms as may be agreed upon by the lending Member and the Company.

(c) Member loans shall be repaid before any distributions are made to Members under Article VIII, unless otherwise agreed by all Members.

## Section 6.5 No Interest on Capital
No Member shall be entitled to receive interest on their Capital Contributions or Capital Account balance.

## Section 6.6 Return of Capital
Except as otherwise provided in this Agreement, no Member shall have the right to demand or receive the return of their Capital Contribution.

# ARTICLE VII: ALLOCATIONS OF PROFITS AND LOSSES

## Section 7.1 Allocation of Profits
Except as otherwise provided in this Agreement, the net profits of the Company for each fiscal year shall be allocated among the Members in proportion to their respective Membership Interests.

## Section 7.2 Allocation of Losses
Except as otherwise provided in this Agreement, the net losses of the Company for each fiscal year shall be allocated among the Members in proportion to their respective Membership Interests.

## Section 7.3 Special Allocations
(a) **Regulatory Allocations.** Notwithstanding the provisions of Sections 7.1 and 7.2, the Company shall make such allocations as are required by the Treasury Regulations under Section 704 of the Internal Revenue Code, including qualified income offset, minimum gain chargeback, and partner nonrecourse debt minimum gain chargeback allocations.

(b) **Curative Allocations.** The Company shall make such curative allocations as are necessary to ensure that the allocations made pursuant to this Article VII have substantial economic effect within the meaning of Treasury Regulations Section 1.704-1(b)(2).

## Section 7.4 Tax Allocations
For income tax purposes, each item of income, gain, loss, and deduction of the Company shall be allocated among the Members in the same manner as the corresponding item of profit or loss is allocated pursuant to this Article VII.

# ARTICLE VIII: DISTRIBUTIONS

## Section 8.1 Distributions Generally
Subject to the provisions of Section 8.3, the Company may make distributions to the Members at such times and in such amounts as the Managing Member shall determine, with the approval of a majority in interest of the Members.

## Section 8.2 Distribution Allocations
All distributions made pursuant to Section 8.1 shall be made to the Members in proportion to their respective Membership Interests.

## Section 8.3 Limitations on Distributions
(a) No distribution shall be made if, after giving effect to the distribution:
   - The Company would not be able to pay its debts as they become due in the usual course of business; or
   - The Company's total assets would be less than the sum of its total liabilities.

(b) The Company shall maintain reasonable reserves for working capital, contingencies, and future business needs before making distributions.

## Section 8.4 Tax Distributions
(a) To the extent funds are available, the Company shall distribute to each Member, no later than March 15 of each year, an amount sufficient to enable each Member to pay federal and state income taxes on the Member's allocable share of the Company's taxable income for the preceding fiscal year.

(b) The amount of such tax distribution shall be calculated by multiplying the Member's allocable share of the Company's taxable income by the highest combined federal and state marginal income tax rate applicable to individuals.

## Section 8.5 Distributions in Kind
The Company may make distributions in kind (i.e., distributions of property other than cash) only with the unanimous consent of all Members. Any such distribution shall be made to all Members in proportion to their Membership Interests.

## Section 8.6 Guaranteed Payments
The Members acknowledge that certain Members may receive guaranteed payments for services rendered to the Company as described in Article IX. Such guaranteed payments shall be treated as expenses of the Company and shall not be considered distributions under this Article VIII.

# ARTICLE XV: TRANSFER OF MEMBERSHIP INTERESTS

## Section 15.1 Restrictions on Transfer
Except as otherwise provided in this Agreement, no Member shall sell, assign, transfer, pledge, hypothecate, or otherwise dispose of ("Transfer") all or any part of their Membership Interest without the prior unanimous written consent of all other Members.

## Section 15.2 Right of First Refusal
(a) Before any Member ("Selling Member") may Transfer any Membership Interest to any person or entity other than another Member or a Permitted Transferee (as defined in Section 15.4), the Selling Member shall first offer such interest to the other Members in accordance with this Section.

(b) The Selling Member shall provide written notice ("Transfer Notice") to the Company and all other Members, which shall include:
   - The identity of the proposed transferee
   - The price and terms of the proposed transfer
   - Copies of all documents relating to the proposed transfer

(c) For a period of thirty (30) days after receipt of the Transfer Notice, the other Members shall have the right, but not the obligation, to purchase all (but not less than all) of the Membership Interest offered, on the same terms and conditions set forth in the Transfer Notice.

(d) The other Members may exercise this right by providing written notice to the Selling Member within the thirty (30) day period. If more than one Member elects to purchase, they shall purchase in proportion to their respective Membership Interests (excluding the Selling Member's interest).

(e) If the other Members do not exercise their right of first refusal, the Selling Member may complete the Transfer to the proposed transferee, but only on terms no more favorable to the transferee than those set forth in the Transfer Notice, and only within ninety (90) days after expiration of the right of first refusal period.

## Section 15.3 Prohibited Transfers
Any Transfer made in violation of this Article XV shall be null and void and of no effect. The Company shall not recognize any such Transfer on its books and records.

## Section 15.4 Permitted Transfers
Notwithstanding Section 15.1, a Member may Transfer their Membership Interest without the consent of the other Members to:

(a) A revocable living trust established by the Member for estate planning purposes, provided the Member is the trustee; or

(b) A Member's spouse or children, provided the transferee agrees in writing to be bound by this Agreement.

Such transfers are "Permitted Transfers," and the transferees are "Permitted Transferees."

## Section 15.5 Admission of Transferee as Member
No transferee of a Membership Interest shall be admitted as a Member unless:

(a) The Transfer was made in compliance with this Article XV

(b) The transferee agrees in writing to be bound by this Agreement

(c) The Members unanimously consent to admit the transferee as a Member

Until admitted as a Member, a transferee shall be entitled only to receive distributions and allocations attributable to the transferred interest, but shall have no voting or other rights.

## Section 15.6 Transfer Expenses
The Selling Member shall bear all costs and expenses incurred by the Company in connection with any Transfer, including legal fees and accounting fees.

# ARTICLE XVI: WITHDRAWAL OF A MEMBER

## Section 16.1 Voluntary Withdrawal
(a) Any Member may voluntarily withdraw from the Company by providing written notice to the other Members at least twelve (12) months in advance of the effective date of withdrawal.

(b) The withdrawing Member shall cooperate fully in the transition of their responsibilities and client relationships to the remaining Members.

## Section 16.2 Effect of Withdrawal
Upon a Member's withdrawal:

(a) The withdrawing Member shall cease to be a Member as of the effective date of withdrawal.

(b) The withdrawing Member's Membership Interest shall be purchased by the remaining Members or the Company in accordance with Article XVIII.

(c) The withdrawing Member shall remain bound by the provisions of Articles XIX (Non-Competition), XX (Confidentiality), and XXI (Intellectual Property).

## Section 16.3 Involuntary Withdrawal
A Member shall be deemed to have involuntarily withdrawn from the Company upon:

(a) The Member's expulsion by unanimous vote of the other Members for:
   - Material breach of this Agreement that remains uncured for thirty (30) days after written notice
   - Conduct that brings the Company into disrepute
   - Conviction of a felony or crime involving moral turpitude
   - Gross negligence or willful misconduct in connection with Company business

(b) Entry of an order for relief against the Member under the Bankruptcy Code

(c) The Member's assignment of their Membership Interest for the benefit of creditors

## Section 16.4 Distributions Upon Withdrawal
A withdrawing Member shall be entitled to receive the fair market value of their Membership Interest, determined and paid in accordance with Article XVIII, less any amounts owed by the withdrawing Member to the Company.

# ARTICLE XVII: DEATH, DISABILITY, OR INCAPACITY

## Section 17.1 Death of a Member
Upon the death of a Member:

(a) The deceased Member's Membership Interest shall be purchased by the remaining Members or the Company in accordance with Article XVIII.

(b) The deceased Member's estate shall be entitled to receive:
   - Any unpaid guaranteed payments through the date of death
   - Any distributions declared but not yet paid
   - The purchase price for the deceased Member's Membership Interest

(c) The remaining Members may, by unanimous consent, admit the deceased Member's heir or beneficiary as a Member in lieu of purchasing the Membership Interest.

## Section 17.2 Disability or Incapacity
(a) "Disability" means a physical or mental condition that prevents a Member from substantially performing their duties under this Agreement for a continuous period of ninety (90) days or for one hundred eighty (180) days in any twelve (12) month period.

(b) Upon the Disability of a Member, the remaining Members may, by majority vote:
   - Continue the disabled Member's guaranteed payments for a period not to exceed six (6) months
   - Reduce or terminate the disabled Member's guaranteed payments
   - Purchase the disabled Member's Membership Interest in accordance with Article XVIII

(c) A Member who becomes disabled may voluntarily withdraw under Section 16.1 without the twelve (12) month notice requirement.

## Section 17.3 Life Insurance
The Company may, but is not required to, obtain life insurance policies on the lives of the Members to fund the purchase of Membership Interests upon death. If such policies are obtained:

(a) The Company shall be the owner and beneficiary of the policies

(b) The premiums shall be paid by the Company as a business expense

(c) Proceeds shall be used to fund the purchase of the deceased Member's Membership Interest

# ARTICLE XVIII: BUY-SELL PROVISIONS

## Section 18.1 Triggering Events
The buy-sell provisions of this Article XVIII shall apply upon the occurrence of any of the following events ("Triggering Events"):

(a) Death of a Member
(b) Disability of a Member
(c) Voluntary withdrawal of a Member
(d) Involuntary withdrawal of a Member
(e) Bankruptcy of a Member
(f) Any other event requiring purchase of a Member's interest under this Agreement

## Section 18.2 Purchase Obligation
Upon the occurrence of a Triggering Event, the remaining Members shall have the obligation (unless waived by unanimous consent) to purchase the departing Member's Membership Interest. The remaining Members shall purchase the interest in proportion to their respective Membership Interests.

## Section 18.3 Valuation

### 18.3.1 Agreed Value Method
The Members shall establish an agreed fair market value of the Company ("Agreed Value") within thirty (30) days after the end of each fiscal year. The Agreed Value shall be documented in writing and signed by all Members.

### 18.3.2 Formula Valuation
If no Agreed Value has been established within the twelve (12) months preceding a Triggering Event, the fair market value of the Company shall be determined as follows:

Fair Market Value = (2.0 x Average Annual Revenue) + (Net Assets - Liabilities)

Where "Average Annual Revenue" means the average of the Company's gross revenues for the three (3) most recent fiscal years (or such shorter period if the Company has been in existence for less than three years).

### 18.3.3 Appraisal
If the departing Member (or their estate) disputes the valuation determined under Section 18.3.2, either party may request an appraisal. The appraisal shall be conducted by a qualified business appraiser mutually agreed upon by the parties. If the parties cannot agree on an appraiser within thirty (30) days, each party shall select an appraiser, and the two appraisers shall select a third appraiser, whose valuation shall be final and binding. The costs of the appraisal shall be borne equally by the departing Member (or estate) and the remaining Members.

## Section 18.4 Purchase Price
The purchase price for a departing Member's Membership Interest shall be:

Purchase Price = Fair Market Value of Company x Departing Member's Membership Interest %

## Section 18.5 Payment Terms

### 18.5.1 Death
If the Triggering Event is death, the purchase price shall be paid as follows:
- Twenty-five percent (25%) in cash within sixty (60) days of death
- The balance in twenty-four (24) equal monthly installments, with interest at the Prime Rate plus two percent (2%)

### 18.5.2 Voluntary Withdrawal
If the Triggering Event is voluntary withdrawal, the purchase price shall be paid in thirty-six (36) equal monthly installments, with interest at the Prime Rate plus two percent (2%).

### 18.5.3 Involuntary Withdrawal
If the Triggering Event is involuntary withdrawal, the purchase price shall be paid in forty-eight (48) equal monthly installments, with interest at the Prime Rate plus two percent (2%).

### 18.5.4 Prepayment
The remaining Members may prepay any installment obligation without penalty.

## Section 18.6 Security
Any installment obligation shall be evidenced by a promissory note. No additional security shall be required.

## Section 18.7 Adjustments
The purchase price may be adjusted (reduced) by:

(a) Any amounts owed by the departing Member to the Company
(b) Any damages suffered by the Company due to the departing Member's breach of this Agreement
(c) Any unperformed obligations of the departing Member

# ARTICLE XIX: NON-COMPETITION AND NON-SOLICITATION

## Section 19.1 Non-Competition During Membership
During the term of a Member's membership in the Company, such Member shall not, directly or indirectly:

(a) Own, manage, operate, control, participate in, or be connected with any business that competes with the Company, except as a passive investor holding less than five percent (5%) of the equity of a publicly traded company

(b) Provide consulting or professional services to any client or prospective client of the Company, except on behalf of the Company

## Section 19.2 Non-Competition After Departure
For a period of two (2) years following the termination of a Member's membership (whether voluntary or involuntary), such former Member shall not, directly or indirectly:

(a) Engage in the business of providing higher education consulting services within the states of Louisiana, Texas, North Carolina, Mississippi, Alabama, or Georgia

(b) Solicit, provide services to, or accept business from any client or prospective client of the Company with whom the Company had a business relationship during the two (2) years preceding the Member's departure

(c) Solicit or attempt to solicit any client or prospective client of the Company for the purpose of providing services competitive with those offered by the Company

## Section 19.3 Non-Solicitation of Employees
For a period of two (2) years following the termination of a Member's membership, such former Member shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit, hire, or attempt to hire any employee, consultant, or contractor of the Company.

## Section 19.4 Non-Solicitation of Members
For a period of two (2) years following the termination of a Member's membership, such former Member shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit or encourage any other Member to leave the Company or to breach this Agreement.

## Section 19.5 Exceptions
The restrictions in this Article XIX shall not apply if:

(a) The Member's departure is due to involuntary withdrawal for reasons other than the Member's breach, misconduct, or fault; or

(b) The remaining Members unanimously consent in writing to waive or modify the restrictions.

## Section 19.6 Reasonableness
The Members acknowledge that:

(a) The restrictions in this Article XIX are reasonable and necessary to protect the Company's legitimate business interests

(b) The restrictions are supported by adequate consideration, including the opportunity to participate in the Company and receive distributions

(c) The restrictions do not impose an undue hardship on any Member

## Section 19.7 Enforcement
If any provision of this Article XIX is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect. If any restriction is held to be overbroad, the court may modify the restriction to make it reasonable and enforceable.

## Section 19.8 Injunctive Relief
The Members acknowledge that breach of this Article XIX would cause irreparable harm to the Company and the other Members for which monetary damages would be inadequate. Accordingly, the Company and the other Members shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief, without the necessity of posting bond, in addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity.

## Section 23.1 Events Causing Dissolution
The Company shall be dissolved upon the occurrence of any of the following events:

(a) The unanimous consent of all Members

(b) The entry of a judicial decree of dissolution

(c) The occurrence of any event that makes it unlawful for the Company's business to continue

(d) The administrative dissolution of the Company by the Texas Secretary of State, unless reinstated

## Section 23.2 No Dissolution Upon Departure
The Company shall not be dissolved upon the death, disability, withdrawal, or expulsion of any Member, provided that at least one Member remains or the remaining Members elect to continue the business.

## Section 23.3 Winding Up
Upon dissolution, the Company's affairs shall be wound up by the Managing Member, or if no Managing Member is then serving, by a liquidating trustee selected by the Members. The winding up shall include:

(a) Collecting all Company assets

(b) Paying or making provision for all Company debts and obligations

(c) Distributing the remaining assets to the Members in accordance with their Capital Account balances

## Section 23.4 Order of Distribution
The assets of the Company shall be distributed in the following order:

(a) First, to creditors of the Company (including Members who are creditors)

(b) Second, to Members in respect of their Capital Account balances

## Section 23.5 Filing of Certificate of Cancellation
Upon completion of the winding up, the Managing Member or liquidating trustee shall file a Certificate of Termination with the Texas Secretary of State.
