# RECITALS

**WHEREAS**, Consultant is engaged in the business of providing higher education consulting services, including but not limited to institutional research, data analytics, student success strategies, strategic planning, and accreditation support;

**WHEREAS**, Client desires to engage Consultant to provide certain professional services from time to time; and

**WHEREAS**, the Parties desire to establish the general terms and conditions under which Consultant will provide services to Client.

# ARTICLE 1: SERVICES

## 1.1 Scope of Services
Consultant agrees to provide professional consulting services to Client as described in one or more Statements of Work ("SOW") executed by the Parties pursuant to this Agreement. Each SOW shall reference this Agreement and shall be subject to all terms and conditions contained herein.

## 1.2 Statements of Work
Each SOW shall include, at a minimum:
- Description of services to be performed
- Deliverables
- Timeline and milestones
- Fees and payment terms
- Specific personnel assigned (if applicable)
- Any terms that modify or supplement this Agreement

## 1.3 Order of Precedence
In the event of any conflict between the terms of this Agreement and a SOW, the terms of the SOW shall control with respect to that particular engagement, unless the SOW expressly states otherwise.

## 1.4 Standard of Performance
Consultant shall perform all services in a professional manner consistent with industry standards and practices. Consultant shall assign qualified personnel with appropriate expertise to perform the services.

---

# ARTICLE 2: TERM AND TERMINATION

## 2.1 Term
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue for an initial term of three (3) years, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article. Thereafter, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive one (1) year terms unless either Party provides written notice of non-renewal at least sixty (60) days prior to the end of the then-current term.

## 2.2 Termination for Convenience
Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience upon sixty (60) days' prior written notice to the other Party.

## 2.3 Termination for Cause
Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party:
- Materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice thereof;
- Becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors; or
- Ceases to conduct business in the normal course.

## 2.4 Effect of Termination
Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement or any SOW:
- Client shall pay Consultant for all services performed and expenses incurred through the effective date of termination;
- Consultant shall deliver to Client all work product completed as of the termination date;
- Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party's Confidential Information;
- The provisions of Articles 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, and 10 shall survive termination.

## 2.5 Suspension
Client may suspend any SOW upon written notice to Consultant. During any suspension period exceeding thirty (30) days, Consultant may reassign personnel and shall not be required to guarantee their availability upon resumption. Resumption of suspended work may require adjustment to timelines and fees.

---

# ARTICLE 3: COMPENSATION AND PAYMENT

## 3.1 Fees
Client shall pay Consultant the fees set forth in each SOW. Unless otherwise specified in a SOW, fees shall be:
- **Fixed Fee Projects:** As specified in the applicable SOW
- **Time and Materials:** At the hourly rates set forth in Exhibit A, as updated from time to time

## 3.2 Expenses
Client shall reimburse Consultant for reasonable out-of-pocket expenses incurred in connection with the services, including but not limited to:
- Travel (airfare, ground transportation, lodging)
- Meals during travel
- Printing and reproduction
- Specialized software or tools required for the engagement

All expenses exceeding $500 individually or $2,500 in aggregate per SOW shall require prior written approval from Client.

## 3.3 Invoicing
Consultant shall submit invoices to Client monthly, or as otherwise specified in the applicable SOW. Invoices shall include:
- Description of services performed
- Hours worked (for time and materials engagements)
- Itemized expenses with receipts for amounts exceeding $25
- Reference to the applicable SOW

## 3.4 Payment Terms
Client shall pay all undisputed invoices within thirty (30) days of receipt. Late payments shall accrue interest at the rate of one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month, or the maximum rate permitted by law, whichever is less.

## 3.5 Disputed Invoices
Client shall notify Consultant in writing of any disputed invoice items within fifteen (15) days of receipt. The Parties shall work in good faith to resolve any disputes. Undisputed portions of invoices shall be paid when due.

## 3.6 Taxes
Fees do not include any applicable sales, use, or other taxes. Client shall be responsible for all such taxes, excluding taxes based on Consultant's income.

# ARTICLE 4: CONFIDENTIALITY

## 4.1 Definition
"Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by one Party ("Disclosing Party") to the other Party ("Receiving Party") in connection with this Agreement, including but not limited to:
- Business strategies, plans, and projections
- Financial information
- Student and employee data
- Technical information and methodologies
- Client lists and relationships
- Any information marked as "Confidential" or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential

## 4.2 Obligations
The Receiving Party agrees to:
- Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence;
- Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party, except to employees, contractors, or agents who have a need to know and are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those herein;
- Use Confidential Information only for purposes of performing under this Agreement;
- Protect Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care used to protect its own confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care.

## 4.3 Exceptions
Confidential Information does not include information that:
- Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party;
- Was rightfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure;
- Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of Confidential Information;
- Is rightfully obtained from a third party without restriction on disclosure;
- Is required to be disclosed by law, provided the Receiving Party gives prompt notice to the Disclosing Party and cooperates in seeking a protective order.

## 4.4 FERPA Compliance
To the extent Consultant receives access to education records as defined by the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA), Consultant agrees to:
- Use such records only for the purposes specified in the applicable SOW;
- Not disclose such records to any third party except as permitted by FERPA;
- Return or destroy such records upon completion of the engagement;
- Comply with all applicable FERPA regulations.

## 4.5 Duration
The obligations of confidentiality shall survive termination of this Agreement for a period of five (5) years, except for trade secrets, which shall be protected indefinitely.

Client retains all right, title, and interest in and to any materials, data, or information provided by Client to Consultant ("Client Materials"). Consultant shall use Client Materials solely for the purpose of performing the services.

## 5.2 Deliverables
Subject to Section 5.3 and upon full payment of all applicable fees, Client shall own all right, title, and interest in and to the deliverables created by Consultant specifically for Client under a SOW ("Deliverables").

## 5.3 Consultant Tools
Consultant retains all right, title, and interest in and to:
- Pre-existing methodologies, frameworks, tools, and know-how ("Consultant Tools");

To the extent any Consultant Tools are incorporated into the Deliverables, Consultant grants Client a non-exclusive, perpetual, royalty-free license to use such Consultant Tools solely as part of the Deliverables.

## 5.4 Feedback
Any feedback, suggestions, or ideas provided by Client regarding Consultant's services or tools may be used by Consultant without restriction or obligation.

## 8.1 Consultant Indemnification
Consultant shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Client and its trustees, officers, employees, and agents from and against any third-party claims, damages, losses, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising from:

- Consultant's breach of confidentiality obligations;

## 8.2 Client Indemnification
Client shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Consultant and its members, officers, employees, and agents from and against any third-party claims, damages, losses, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising from:

# ARTICLE 10: GENERAL PROVISIONS

## 10.1 Independent Contractor
Consultant is an independent contractor. Nothing in this Agreement shall create an employment, partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship between the Parties. Consultant shall be solely responsible for all taxes, withholdings, and other statutory obligations related to its personnel.

## 10.2 Assignment
Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other Party, except that either Party may assign this Agreement to an affiliate or in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of substantially all of its assets. Any purported assignment in violation of this section shall be void.

## 10.3 Notices
All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and shall be deemed delivered when:
- Delivered personally;
- Sent by email with confirmation of receipt;
- Three (3) days after being sent by certified mail, return receipt requested;
- One (1) day after being sent by overnight courier.

Notices shall be sent to the addresses set forth on the signature page or to such other addresses as a Party may designate in writing.

## 10.4 Force Majeure
Neither Party shall be liable for any delay or failure in performance resulting from causes beyond its reasonable control, including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, war, terrorism, pandemic, government actions, labor disputes, or internet or telecommunications failures. The affected Party shall promptly notify the other Party and use reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact.

## 10.5 Waiver
No waiver of any provision of this Agreement shall be effective unless in writing and signed by the waiving Party. No waiver shall constitute a waiver of any other provision or a continuing waiver.

## 10.6 Severability
If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect. The invalid provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid and enforceable.

## 10.7 Entire Agreement
This Agreement, together with all SOWs and Exhibits, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior agreements, understandings, negotiations, and discussions.

## 10.8 Amendments
This Agreement may be amended only by a written instrument signed by both Parties.

## 10.9 Governing Law
This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Louisiana, without regard to its conflict of laws principles.

## 10.10 Dispute Resolution
Any dispute arising under this Agreement shall be resolved as follows:
1. Good faith negotiation between the Parties;
2. Mediation by a mutually agreed-upon mediator;
3. Binding arbitration in accordance with the rules of the American Arbitration Association, conducted in Louisiana.

## 10.11 Counterparts
This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original. Electronic signatures shall be deemed valid and binding.

## 10.12 Publicity
Neither Party shall use the other Party's name, logo, or trademarks in any publicity or marketing materials without prior written consent, except that Consultant may include Client's name in a general list of clients.
