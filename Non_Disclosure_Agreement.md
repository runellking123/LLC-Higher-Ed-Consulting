# ARTICLE 1: DEFINITIONS

## 1.1 "Confidential Information"
"Confidential Information" means any and all non-public information disclosed by one Party ("Disclosing Party") to the other Party ("Receiving Party"), whether disclosed orally, in writing, electronically, or by any other means, including but not limited to:

**For Elevate EdStrategy:**
- Methodologies, frameworks, and proprietary processes
- Pricing information and fee structures
- Client lists and relationships
- Business strategies and plans
- Software, tools, and templates
- Employee and contractor information

**For [Short Name]:**
- Student records and education data
- Employee and personnel information
- Financial information and budgets
- Strategic plans and initiatives
- Proprietary data and research
- Information technology systems and security protocols

## 1.2 "Representatives"
"Representatives" means a Party's employees, officers, directors, members, partners, agents, attorneys, accountants, consultants, contractors, and advisors who have a need to know the Confidential Information for the Purpose and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those in this Agreement.

---

# ARTICLE 2: CONFIDENTIALITY OBLIGATIONS

## 2.1 Protection of Confidential Information
The Receiving Party agrees to:

(a) Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence;

(b) Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party, except to its Representatives as permitted herein;

(c) Use Confidential Information solely for the Purpose and not for any other purpose;

(d) Protect Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care used to protect its own confidential information of similar nature, but in no event less than reasonable care;

(e) Limit access to Confidential Information to those Representatives who have a need to know for the Purpose;

(f) Inform its Representatives of the confidential nature of the information and ensure their compliance with this Agreement;

(g) Promptly notify the Disclosing Party of any unauthorized use or disclosure of Confidential Information.

## 2.2 No Copies
The Receiving Party shall not copy or reproduce Confidential Information except as reasonably necessary for the Purpose.

## 2.3 No Reverse Engineering
The Receiving Party shall not reverse engineer, disassemble, or decompile any Confidential Information, including any software, prototypes, or samples.

---

# ARTICLE 3: EXCLUSIONS

## 3.1 Exceptions
Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:

(a) Was publicly known at the time of disclosure or becomes publicly known through no fault of the Receiving Party;

(b) Was rightfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party, as evidenced by written records;

(c) Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without reference to or use of the Confidential Information, as evidenced by written records;

(d) Is rightfully received from a third party without restriction on disclosure and without breach of any obligation to the Disclosing Party;

(e) Is approved for release by prior written authorization of the Disclosing Party.

## 3.2 Required Disclosures
If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or legal process (including subpoena or court order) to disclose any Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall:

(a) Promptly notify the Disclosing Party of such requirement (to the extent legally permitted);

(b) Cooperate with the Disclosing Party's efforts to obtain a protective order or other appropriate remedy;

(c) Disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that is legally required;

(d) Use reasonable efforts to ensure that the disclosed information is treated confidentially.

---

# ARTICLE 4: FERPA AND DATA PROTECTION

## 4.1 Education Records
To the extent any Confidential Information includes education records as defined by the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA), 20 U.S.C. ยง 1232g, and its implementing regulations, 34 C.F.R. Part 99, the Receiving Party agrees to:

(a) Use such records only for the Purpose as specified herein;

(b) Not re-disclose such records to any third party without prior written consent of the Disclosing Party, except as permitted by FERPA;

(c) Not use such records for any purpose other than the Purpose for which disclosure was made;

(d) Implement appropriate administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect such records;

(e) Return or destroy such records upon completion of the Purpose or termination of this Agreement.

## 4.2 Data Security
Each Party shall implement and maintain reasonable security measures to protect Confidential Information from unauthorized access, use, or disclosure, including:

(a) Encryption of electronic Confidential Information in transit and at rest;

(b) Access controls limiting access to authorized personnel;

(c) Secure storage of physical documents containing Confidential Information;

(d) Secure disposal of Confidential Information when no longer needed.

## 4.3 Security Breach Notification
In the event of any actual or suspected unauthorized access to, use of, or disclosure of Confidential Information ("Security Breach"), the affected Party shall:

(a) Notify the other Party within forty-eight (48) hours of discovering the Security Breach;

(b) Investigate the Security Breach and take appropriate remedial measures;

(c) Cooperate with the other Party in investigating and addressing the Security Breach;

(d) Provide reasonable assistance in any required notifications to affected individuals or regulatory authorities.

# ARTICLE 5: OWNERSHIP AND NO LICENSE

## 5.1 Ownership
All Confidential Information shall remain the exclusive property of the Disclosing Party. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as granting any rights, license, or interest in or to any Confidential Information, except the limited right to use such information for the Purpose.

## 5.2 No Implied License
No license, express or implied, is granted under any patent, trademark, copyright, trade secret, or other intellectual property right of either Party by this Agreement or by any disclosure of Confidential Information hereunder.

## 5.3 No Obligation
Nothing in this Agreement shall obligate either Party to:

(a) Enter into any further agreement or business relationship;

(b) Disclose any particular Confidential Information;

(c) Purchase or sell any products or services;

(d) Refrain from entering into agreements with third parties.

---

# ARTICLE 6: RETURN OF MATERIALS

## 6.1 Return or Destruction
Upon the earlier of (a) termination or expiration of this Agreement, (b) completion of the Purpose, or (c) written request by the Disclosing Party, the Receiving Party shall promptly:

(a) Return to the Disclosing Party all tangible materials containing Confidential Information;

(b) Destroy all electronic copies of Confidential Information in its possession or control;

(c) Certify in writing, upon request, that all Confidential Information has been returned or destroyed.

## 6.2 Exceptions
Notwithstanding Section 6.1, the Receiving Party may retain:

(a) Copies of Confidential Information to the extent required by law or regulation;

(b) Archived copies maintained in accordance with standard backup procedures, provided such copies remain subject to the confidentiality obligations of this Agreement.

---

# ARTICLE 7: TERM AND TERMINATION

## 7.1 Term
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue for a period of two (2) years, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Agreement.

## 7.2 Termination
Either Party may terminate this Agreement at any time upon thirty (30) days' prior written notice to the other Party.

## 7.3 Survival
The obligations of confidentiality set forth in this Agreement shall survive termination or expiration of this Agreement for a period of five (5) years, except for trade secrets, which shall be protected for as long as they remain trade secrets under applicable law.

---

# ARTICLE 8: REMEDIES

## 8.1 Injunctive Relief
The Parties acknowledge that any breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm to the Disclosing Party for which monetary damages may be inadequate. Accordingly, in addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity, the Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief, specific performance, or other equitable relief, without the necessity of proving actual damages or posting any bond.

## 8.2 Indemnification
Each Party agrees to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the other Party from and against any and all claims, damages, losses, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising from any breach of this Agreement by the indemnifying Party or its Representatives.

---

## 9.1 Governing Law

## 9.2 Jurisdiction
Any legal action or proceeding arising under this Agreement shall be brought exclusively in the state or federal courts located in Texas, and the Parties hereby consent to the personal jurisdiction and venue of such courts.

## 9.3 Entire Agreement
This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior and contemporaneous agreements, understandings, negotiations, and discussions.

## 9.4 Amendment
This Agreement may be amended only by a written instrument signed by both Parties.

## 9.5 Waiver
No waiver of any provision of this Agreement shall be effective unless in writing and signed by the waiving Party. No waiver shall constitute a waiver of any other provision or a continuing waiver.

## 9.6 Severability
If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

## 9.7 Assignment
Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other Party, except to an affiliate or in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of substantially all of its assets.

## 9.8 Notices
All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and shall be deemed delivered when delivered personally, sent by email with confirmation of receipt, or one (1) business day after being sent by overnight courier, to the addresses set forth on the signature page.

## 9.9 Counterparts
This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original. Electronic signatures shall be deemed valid and binding.

## 9.10 No Partnership
Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to create a partnership, joint venture, employment, or agency relationship between the Parties.
